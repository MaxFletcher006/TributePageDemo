Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela (18 July 1918 – 5 December 2013) was a South African anti-apartheid activist who served as the first president of South Africa from 1994 to 1999. He was the country's first black head of state and the first elected in a fully representative democratic election. His government focused on dismantling the legacy of apartheid by fostering racial reconciliation. Ideologically an African nationalist and socialist, he served as the president of the African National Congress (ANC) party from 1991 to 1997.

Here is a timeline of Nelson Mandela

  • 1918, July 18th - Born in Mvezo, Union of South Africa as Rolihlahla Mandela
  • 1925 - Attends primary school near Qunu (recieves the name 'Nelson' from a teahcer)
  • 1930 - Attends Clarkeburry Boarding Institute in Engcobo
  • 1937 - Attends Healdtown, the Wesleyan College at Fort Beaufor
  • 1939 - Enrols at the University Collegeo of Fort Hare, in Alice
  • 1942 - Completes BA through the University of South Africa (UNISA)
  • 1942 - Begins to attend African National Congress (ANC) meeting informally
  • 1948 - Elected national secretary of the ANCYL
  • 1951 - Elected President of the ANCYL
  • 1956, December 5th - Arrested and later joins 155 others on trial for treason
  • 1962, January 11th - Leaves the country for military training and to garner support for the ANC
  • 1962, July 23rd - Returns to South Africa
  • 1962, August 5th - Arrested near Howick in KwaZulu-Natal
  • 1962, November 7th - Sentenced to five years in prison for incitement and leaving the country without a passport
  • 1963, May 27th - Sent to Robben Island
  • 1990, February 2nd - ANC is unbanned
  • 1990, February 11th - Released
  • 1993, December 10th - Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize with President F.W. de Klerk
  • 1994, April 27th - Votes for the first time in his life
  • 1994, May 9th - Elected by Parliament as first president of democratic South Africa
  • 1994, May 10th - Inaugurated as President of the Republic of South Africa
  • 1999 - Steps down after one term as President, establishes the Nelson Mandela Foundation
  • 2003 - Establishes the Mandela Rhodes Foundation
  • 2013, December 5th - Passes away at home in Johannesburg
